# Community Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The AI Trust Receipt project depends on rigorous disagreement, inspectable reasoning, and professional treatment of contributors and affected communities. Participation should improve the quality of the specification, implementation, evidence, or public understanding without turning technical disagreement into personal conflict.

## Expected conduct

Participants are expected to:

- engage with claims, evidence, tests, and design tradeoffs rather than attacking people;
- distinguish observed behavior from inference, allegation, endorsement, and proof;
- identify uncertainty and limitations when evidence is incomplete;
- respect privacy, confidentiality, intellectual contribution, and security-reporting boundaries;
- avoid discriminatory, threatening, harassing, humiliating, or sexually inappropriate conduct;
- avoid persistent unwanted contact or public disclosure of another person's private information;
- give accurate attribution and identify material modifications;
- disclose conflicts of interest that may affect a technical or governance position;
- preserve the project's candidate-status boundary and avoid implying certification, adoption, or regulatory approval;
- accept correction when a claim, test, or implementation is shown to be inaccurate.

## Unacceptable conduct

Unacceptable conduct includes:

- harassment, intimidation, stalking, threats, or discriminatory abuse;
- doxxing, exposure of private receipt data, credentials, or security-sensitive information;
- fabricated evidence, manipulated test results, or knowingly false claims of conformance;
- use of project participation to imply endorsement of a product, organization, audit, certification, or deployment;
- coordinated disruption, spam, bad-faith issue flooding, or repeated derailment;
- retaliation against a person who reports a security, conduct, governance, or integrity concern in good faith.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, release processes, project events, and public communications made in an official project capacity.

## Reporting

Conduct concerns may be reported privately to:

**brittanywright@emotionalinfrastructure.org**

Security vulnerabilities should follow `SECURITY.md`.

A report should include the relevant location, date, participants, conduct at issue, available evidence, and any immediate safety or privacy concern. Do not circulate confidential information more widely than necessary to evaluate the report.

## Enforcement

The maintainer may edit or remove content, limit participation, lock a conversation, reject a contribution, issue a warning, or suspend or permanently restrict participation when conduct materially undermines safety, integrity, or productive project work.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately or publicly as appropriate, and avoid unnecessary disclosure of sensitive information. Retaliation for good-faith reporting is itself a violation.

## Good-faith disagreement

A technically forceful critique is not misconduct merely because it challenges a proposal, implementation, or claim. The relevant distinction is whether the critique is evidence-based, directed at the work, and conducted without harassment, deception, or abuse.
